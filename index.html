01

Mission

Why
We
Exist

"A world where democracy is the rule, not the exception — where leaders govern with accountability, inclusion, and purpose."

The Club of Madrid Foundation Inc. (COMFI) was incorporated in 2004 as the US-based, tax-exempt fundraising arm of Club de Madrid. We exist to channel philanthropic support from American donors directly into the charitable and educational activities of Club de Madrid.

Club de Madrid brings together more than 130 distinguished former presidents and prime ministers from over 74 nations. These leaders volunteer their expertise, credibility, and global networks on a pro bono basis to support democratic transitions, resolve political conflicts, and counsel governments at critical crossroads.

As a non-partisan, international organization, Club de Madrid operates at the highest level of political engagement. COMFI makes it possible for US-based donors to be part of that mission with full tax deductibility.

02

Programs

01
Democratic Leadership

Peer-to-peer engagement between former and current heads of state to build democratic institutions, constitutional frameworks, and the rule of law in countries at critical inflection points.

02
Crisis & Post-Crisis Response

Deploying the credibility of Club de Madrid members in conflict-affected nations to facilitate political dialogue, broker ceasefires, and build durable foundations for peace.

03
Shared Societies

Advancing social cohesion and inclusion through evidence-based policy recommendations that help diverse populations coexist peacefully within democratic frameworks.

04
Policy Dialogue

Annual working groups bringing together members, scholars, and civil society to develop actionable recommendations on challenges from economic inequality to climate governance.

05
Sustainable Development

Advocacy aligned with the UN's SDGs, with emphasis on equitable global tax policy, climate action, and economic development for the Global South.

06
Education & Research

Publications and research documenting lessons from democratic transitions, made accessible to governments, practitioners, scholars, and civil society worldwide.
