Mission

Who
We
Are

"Democratic governance is not a destination — it is a practice, sustained by leadership, accountability, and the courage to engage across difference."

The Club of Madrid Foundation Inc. (COMFI) is a US-based, independent 501(c)(3) foundation incorporated in 2004. Our mission is to advance the strengthening of democratic governance worldwide — primarily through our close partnership with Club de Madrid, and through our own convening, philanthropic, and advocacy work.

We are distinct from Club de Madrid, though deeply aligned with its mission. COMFI raises philanthropic capital from US-based donors and institutions, channels that support into Club de Madrid's international programs, and independently develops initiatives that advance democracy, accountability, and the rule of law — particularly at the intersection of business, policy, and global governance.

Our Partner

Club de Madrid is the world's largest forum of democratically elected former heads of state and government — 133 members from more than 70 nations. These leaders contribute their credibility, expertise, and networks on a pro bono basis to support democratic transitions, mediate political conflicts, and advise governments at critical moments. COMFI makes it possible for US donors to support this mission with full tax deductibility.

Our Own Work

Beyond our fundraising role, COMFI independently convenes senior leaders from government, business, and civil society to advance substantive dialogue on democracy's most pressing challenges — from energy governance and critical supply chains to democratic backsliding and the future of multilateral institutions. Our annual UNGA Dinner Series is a flagship example of this convening mission.

02

Programs & Areas of Focus

01
Democratic Leadership

Peer-to-peer engagement between former and sitting heads of state to strengthen democratic institutions, constitutional frameworks, and the rule of law in countries navigating critical political transitions.

02
Business & Democracy Dialogue

Substantive convening at the intersection of private sector leadership and democratic governance — including the annual UNGA Dinner Series — advancing public-private dialogue on energy, critical minerals, geopolitical risk, and responsible investment.

03
Crisis & Post-Crisis Response

Deploying the moral authority and experience of Club de Madrid members in conflict-affected contexts to facilitate political dialogue, support ceasefires, and build durable foundations for democratic recovery.

04
Shared Societies

Advancing social cohesion through evidence-based policy work that helps diverse populations coexist — and thrive — within pluralistic, democratic frameworks.

05
Sustainable Development & Global Governance

Advocacy aligned with the UN's Sustainable Development Goals, with particular focus on equitable global tax policy, climate governance, and economic development in the Global South — where democratic governance and sustainable growth are inextricably linked.

06
Research & Knowledge Sharing

Publications, case studies, and policy briefs documenting lessons from democratic transitions — made accessible to governments, practitioners, scholars, and civil society to inform the next generation of democratic leadership.

One of the most substantive private gatherings of UN General Assembly week, the UNGA Dinner brings together democratically elected former heads of state with senior investors and industry leaders for an open and dynamic exchange on the geopolitical challenges defining the global agenda. This year's program focuses on energy and critical minerals — the vital inputs underpinning modern economies, democratic competition, and national security.

Panel I · Industry & Business
Meeting Global Needs: Challenges in Energy & Critical Minerals for the 21st Century Economy
Executives and practitioners from energy, AI, defense, consumer electronics, batteries, semiconductors, and advanced manufacturing — exploring governance gaps, supply chain resilience, ethical sourcing, and public-private partnership models that balance commercial returns with social and ecological accountability.
Panel II · Club de Madrid Members
Meeting Societal Needs: Democratic Governance, Market Competition, and Geopolitical Risk
Democratically elected leaders from Club de Madrid's network of 133 members across 70+ countries — addressing governance reforms, multilateral cooperation, regional supply diversification, ESG operationalization, and the role of democratic governments in combatting illicit extraction and corruption.
