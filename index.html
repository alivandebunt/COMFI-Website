Our Purpose

Why COMFI Exists

"A world where democracy is the rule, not the exception — where leaders govern with accountability, inclusion, and purpose."

The Club of Madrid Foundation Inc. (COMFI) was incorporated in 2004 as the US-based, tax-exempt fundraising arm of Club de Madrid. We exist to channel philanthropic support from American donors directly into the charitable and educational activities of Club de Madrid.

Club de Madrid — the parent organization — brings together more than 130 distinguished former presidents and prime ministers from over 74 nations. These leaders volunteer their expertise, credibility, and global networks on a pro bono basis to support democratic transitions, resolve political conflicts, and counsel governments navigating critical crossroads.

As a non-partisan, international organization, Club de Madrid operates at the highest level of political engagement — peer-to-peer dialogue between current and former world leaders. COMFI makes it possible for US-based donors to be part of that mission with full tax deductibility.

What We Support

Key Program Areas

01

Democratic Leadership

Supporting peer-to-peer engagement between former and current heads of state to build and consolidate democratic institutions, constitutional frameworks, and rule of law.

02

Crisis & Post-Crisis Response

Deploying the influence of Club de Madrid members in crisis-affected countries to facilitate political dialogue, support ceasefires, and build the foundations for peace.

03

Shared Societies

Advancing the Shared Societies Project — promoting social cohesion, inclusion, and policies that help diverse populations live together in peace within democratic frameworks.

04

Policy Dialogue

Convening annual policy dialogues that bring together members, scholars, and civil society to produce actionable recommendations on issues from inequality to climate governance.

05

Sustainable Development

Working toward the UN's Sustainable Development Goals, including advocacy on global tax policy, climate action, and equitable economic development for the Global South.

06

Education & Research

Funding educational initiatives, publications, and research that document lessons from democratic transitions and make them accessible to governments, scholars, and civil society worldwide.
